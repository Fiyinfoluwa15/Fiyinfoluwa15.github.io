What colleagues have to say about me?

This is feedback I have gathered from colleagues I have worked with.

1. Michael, International Promotions manager || International recruitment team

What my team needed help with:

In international recruitment, data is the backbone of our strategy, influencing which markets we enter or which partnerships we dissolve. We needed a dynamic dashboard to track application trends by country throughout the year. Our goal was to visualize the direct impact of scholarship announcements, policy changes, and economic shifts on our recruitment efforts in real-time.

How Fiyin helped:

Fiyin was exceptionally intentional in her approach. She took the time to deeply understand our workflow before suggesting technical solutions - a rare and appreciated quality in a data partner. She translated our "layman’s terms" into high-quality technical outputs and demonstrated impressive critical thinking. For instance, she steered us away from using confusing week numbers toward a month-based view. This simple recommendation significantly improved data accessibility for new team members and made aligning strategic changes with results much more intuitive.

Project Impact:

The dashboard has revolutionized how we align initiatives with recruitment peaks. It revealed insights that were previously hidden in our historical data. For example, we could clearly see the direct correlation between our January 2025 Africa initiative and the resulting spike in applications across specific academic schools.

2. Gill, Analyst developer || Data architecture team

What my team needed help with:

The project was to convert SSRS reports for SDS into Qlik reports. This required liaison with the data engineer to convert the SQL to Qlik data, and then Fiy’s role was to put these into Qlik. The report requirements were not clearly written, and had never properly been gathered from users, nor had the SSRS reports been completed before they had been abandoned some months before for operational reasons. I worked with Fiy to try to anticipate users’ requirements and translate what information might be needed in each report. Fiy not having access to Cressida complicated matters unnecessarily. Although screenshots were provided, it was hard for Fiy to check data quality.

How Fiyin helped:

Fiy was very approachable and listened carefully to what was required given the constraints we were working under. She dealt with the work promptly and efficiently. It seemed to work best when discussing changes and updating Qlik in person due to the nature of the work and because some data fields had changed name between the source and the Qlik fields. Working this way helped to avoid any errors in understanding.

Project Impact:

The target was to produce working reports which Fiy delivered on time and working with limited requirement information and access to source data.
